The Project

This was a 4-week project to design a new hypothetical product for LinkedIn to diversify their userbase, by providing individuals in declining industries with help switching careers.

The outcome was a new product offering on LinkedIn that helps individuals see what careers they may be suited for based on their transferable skills and extends LinkedInâ€™s mentorship program by connecting them to mentors who made a switch out of the same career.

My Role

I worked on content strategy, prototype copywriting, interaction design, and UI animations. This project also showcases my ability in production, as I was responsible for creating a product video showcasing an animated userflow through our solution for presentation to our audience.

Initial Research

In the United States, trades workers outnumber knowledge workers by 253%. However, only 11.5% of LinkedIn members work in the trades industries.

With automation increasingly replacing jobs in trades industries, we found an opening for LinkedIn to diversity its userbase by providing support for trades workers interested in switching to a more future-proof career.

In comparison to job searching sites like Indeed or Glassdoor which rely on aggregating job postings, LinkedIn's strength is its unique professional networking format. Our user interviews revealed that trades workers are commonly hired through referrals from family, friends and colleagues. This made LinkedIn the optimal platform to engage with these workers.

Framing

How might we leverage LinkedIn's unique social networking platform to provide support to workers in trades industries affected by automation in switching careers?

Persona

When creating our persona, we were aware of the limitations of our solution and realized that it was unlikely we could design a digital intervention to immediately help someone who had just been laid off from their job. However, we did see the potential to support individuals who were pro-actively searching for opportunities to shift into a more stable career, and we aimed to target these individuals early on in their searching.

Ideating + Sketching

Why Mobile?

Our first iteration of our solution was for desktop. However, research showed that individuals in manual labour occupations were more likely to use mobile devices to search for jobs, because their jobs require them to be on their feet and moving around rather than sitting behind a desk (source).

As a result, we chose to create our app for mobile-first.

Mentor Profile + Timeline

I adapted our original desktop profile and timeline screens for mobile. I created sketch wireframes of the mobile interfaces as well as how the user might interact with the elements and move through the page. Because there was so much information on the timeline, I introduced an element of horizontal scrolling to break up the amount of scrolling down.

Smart Chat

While iterating on the form of the smart chat, I sketched out multiple wireframes for the interface, as well as the content. Initially, I considered putting tasks inside the pop-up prompts, relating to the topic. However, this had the risk of disrupting the conversation as a potential distraction. In the end, we decided to keep the information contained to the chat window.

Prototype + UI Animations

Suggested Careers

As the user fills in each question, the next section slides up from the bottom, pushing the previous section out of the way. This calls the user's focus to the next section, showing a clear path of how to progress.

By having the questions appear one at a time, this reduces the cognitive overload for the user.

Search Results

When users choose one or more careers to explore, they are shown a list of LinkedIn mentors who switched from their career to the one they selected.

The names and profile pictures are hidden on this page, in accordance with LinkedIn's privacy policy. After creating an account or logging into an existing one, users can see a detailed profile.

Mentor Profile

At the top of the mentor profile is a brief welcome text written by the mentor to make the profile feel more personal. Users can explore the profile further, and/or get into contact with them.

Included are transferrable skills and a timeline of events on the profile. This helps the user get a glimpse of how their own skills might be transferrable, and other actions they might take.

Timeline

Similar to the welcome text, I included quotes from the mentor about significant events in order to humanize the profile.

Timeline events pop up one at a time as I wanted to highlight a sense of discovery and playfulness when viewing the timeline, to show career switching as something exciting, not scary.

Product Video

At the end of the project, I was responsible for creating the full animated userflow through our product, and producing a product video for presentation to our audience.

I used Adobe After Effects to create our solution user flow. I began by disassembling the screens in Figma in order to export each interactive or moving part individually. I kept these organized in Figma by collecting together screen bases, interactive components, and different interaction states.

Branding

To ensure that our video was consistent with LinkedIn's other product offering videos, I looked at LinkedIn's Youtube channel for inspiration. This can be seen in the use of their signature blue colour for the background, and the shortened, white logo instead of using the full logotype.

Voiceover

I wrote the script for the voiceover, and used a Zoom H4N recorder along with a shotgun mike to direct and record in a sound studio with our voice actor. After uploading the audio files, I used the audio mixer in Premiere Pro to boost the sound quality.

Music

We wanted to position our solution as a glimmer of hope for individuals who don't know how to move forward from their declining industry. As a result, I took care to select music that would match this tone; not too upbeat and cheerful, but also not too sombre. Furthermore, it should not be too complex-sounding with many highs and lows, as that could be a distraction from the content.

In the end, I chose Lights by Sappheiros because of its optimistic, yet low-key sound. Because the rhythmn is a repeating beat, it easily fades into the background.

Reflection

This project was my first time creating UI animations and micro-interactions. I realized the importance of keeping all the assets organized in both the prototyping program as well as After Effects, which made it much easier to edit and iterate on the go without wasting time locating resources. I was also able to develop my technical skills in After Effects.

There were both benefits and downsides to sticking strictly with our client's branding and interface patterns. The benefit was becoming familiar with operating with established branding guidelines for consistency, which would be the most realistic scenario in an established company. The downside was that we had less freedom to explore visual design in this project.
