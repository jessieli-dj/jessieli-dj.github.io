The Project

I led a team of 5 in 2-week project, ending in a 2-day design competition in Seattle, where we placed 2nd out of 14 teams. We were tasked with creating with an architectural form, presented in a well-designed slide deck. The concept behind our architectural model was inspired by the flight paths that airplanes create when moving through the sky. This led to our concept of intersecting paths that create a forward motion.

My Role

I worked closely with all team members to manage the project parts for consistency and was also responsible for content strategy and visual design for the slide deck, through the thoughtful use of typeface, graphics, colours, and image treatment.

Graphic Composition

Type Selection

I was responsible for choosing the typefaces that would be used to present our architectural model in our slide deck. Because we were presenting on screen to a large audience, I decided that a sans serif typeface would be best for legibility. I compared approximately 30 sans serif typefaces to find one that could be represent our project.

In the end, I narrowed our choices down to two sets of typefaces: Futura or Overpass for headers, and Din Pro or HK Grotesk for body copy.

I chose Overpass over Futura for headers because of how uniform the shapes were in Futura which made the typeface feel more static on the page. On the other hand, Overpass is commonly used as the typeface on highway road signs as its dynamic composition makes it more eye catching from a distance.

For body copy, I chose HK Grotesk over Din Pro because of the more dynamic lines in the characters. I found that though both were very legible, Din Pro was more of a standard sans serif. On the other hand, HK Grotesk's elegant adaptation of a classic serif lowercase “g” made it stand out against other sans serif fonts.

Colour Palette

I was inspired by the black and white tarmac of airport runways, with blinking red lights that guide planes to either take off or land. I chose images with heavy contrast in colours so that when converted to black and white, they would stand out against the light gray background. Because we wanted to highlight parts of the architecture that inspired our form, I used a dark pink-red for guiding lines to stand out against the monochromatic slides.

Reflection

Due to the tight deadlines, I developed several time management techniques and team facilitation skills. This included checking in regularly to make sure we were on the same page, setting a schedule each week to avoid burn out, and facilitating debrief discussions to maintain a positive working environment.

I learned a lot about lateral thinking and working in this project. As we continued to refine and change our concept and form, I was also refining the visual design of the slide deck at the same time. I made sure not to get too attached to any particular part of the project, which allowed us to iterate rapidly based on feedback.

An area of improvement that I took away is to be less literal with art direction. We used airplane and airport imagery to inspire our art direction. While it worked for the most part as I abstracted out the ideas and adapted it for our visual design, some slides took it too far and became a literal representation of an airport runway.
