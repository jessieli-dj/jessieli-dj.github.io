Nokia Internship

Overview

I worked closely with a senior interaction designer to create an experience around leveraging automation to help network engineers save time and reduce risk of human error.

This experience was brought to life through the close collaboration of a multidisciplinary team composed of designers, developers, QA, and product manager.

Skills

  • Interaction design
  • interface design
  • documentation
  • user research

Tools

  • Sketch
  • InVision
  • Jira

When

May - December 2019

What I Learned

Using judgment to balance desireability, viability and feasibility

I applied design thinking to create design recommendations that provide clear value for the user and business. I also communicated consistently with the product team to stay updated on technical limitations and make judgment calls to ensure feasibility for production.

Communicating for successful multidisciplinary collaborations

I used my communication and problem-solving skills to set expectations frequently and clearly to keep up with changing requirements. This helped reduce miscommunications that might have taken us off the project road, and allowed us to manage our timelines effectively.

Adapting to designing for a highly technical domain and user

Having started with almost no domain knowledge, I used my troubleshooting skills to find information using internal and external resources, and took initiative to ask questions about things I didn’t understand. As a result, I became productive in time to take on work for the next product release.

My Nokia internship exceeded my expectations. I can’t wait to take what I’ve learned into the next stage of my design journey. Thanks to the Nokia ION UX team for a great 8 months!
