There were many different elements of the page that competed in importance and so the ultimate goal was to find the right balance. I listened closely to our subject expertsâ€™ feedback, while also referring to the competitors that I studied at the start and providing a user-focused perspective. Coin Filter

My initial design included coin type filters as there could be many. However, stakeholders felt that it was too hidden. One business objective was to expose the amount of coins we supported, which could increase trade as customers usually traded in a few specific coins. I revised the design with that in mind. The choice chips acted as tabs, displaying a few chips by default and the user could scroll to discover more. This allowed us to address the most common use cases while also supporting edge cases.

Market Pricing and Actions

My first proposal was to have minimal information on the Market screen and click into a specific coin to see the pricing and action buttons. However, stakeholders felt this was a level of interaction that might cause user drop-off before actually placing an order. On their advice, I explored options of exposing the price and call-to-actions earlier in the flow.

The final design we landed on was a nice compromise. Users could see the price and go directly to placing an order from the Market screen but only once they have expanded the coin theyâ€™re interested in. This avoided overwhelming the user with too much information as soon as they opened the app while still speeding up their workflow.
